Navigating the Course

  • Attendance: Attendance is highly encouraged for this class, not because of grades, but because this course works best when our discussions include a wide array of differing perspectives. That being said, you'll notice there is no attendance policy in this class. You will always have the opportunity to make up assignments you miss in class. The goal of my approach to attendance is to (try to) ensure that people are present because they want to be.
  • Assignments: Many of our assignments involve your own reflections and are scored by completion, rather than evaluated by grades. There are, however, some assignments that are evaluated based on the traditional A-F grading scale, such as the final project and the written analyses. Beyond that, the key to success is simply completing all the major and minor assignments to the best of your ability (while noting that some of our assignments will not require much of you).
  • Discussions: You will often hear me express how important it is for you to contribute to our class discussions. Although it is true that your contributions will likely be strengthened and complexified by doing the readings, I want to encourage you to contribute even if you didn't have time to sit with our readings before class. While I am more than happy to lecture about media criticism for hours on end, I believe we all will benefit from hearing many different voices and considering many different perspectives in the class.
  • Readings: The readings are assigned to help prep you for the in-class conversation. Admittedly, I'm currently wrestling with the importance of readings since I've received feedback that many students simply do not read for class. While doing these readings will provide you with more nuance and more time to sit with these concepts, I don't want to exclude students who didn't have enough time to really sit with each reading before class. If you are able, I would ask that you really think about these readings and bring questions and commentary related to the readings to each class.
  • Differences of Opinion: We should have differences of opinion as we talk through course concepts this semester. Even if we agree on the basics, we will each have differing reactions to the various methods we'll learn about in class. Some people, for example, will be more moved by psychoanalysis, while some will be fascinated by postmodernism. Our class sessions will be enriched by your contributions, so I am asking you to please share your perspectives, even if they differ from mine or from your classmates'. I want to ensure that a multiplicity of perspectives are represented in the room so we can learn to see the world through different frameworks.

Navigating the Canvas Shell

  • Modules: The modules contain all the readings and assignments due in a given week. Additionally, the module for the corresponding class period includes a copy of the PowerPoint presentation given in class. 
  • Announcements: Throughout the semester, I will post several announcements with information about the class or about on-campus events. Please be sure to check your settings to make sure you receive an email when new announcements are posted.
  • Assignments: Most assignments will be listed in the Modules, but it's a good idea to check this page to ensure you're keeping up to date. 
  • Discussions: We will use the Canvas discussion boards when analyzing certain texts, so be sure to contribute to those discussions, even if you weren't in class that day.
  • Grades: It's a great idea to keep an eye on your grades to make sure my records match your expectations. Canvas also lets you test out certain scores so you can get an idea of your overall grade based on your projected performance on an upcoming assignment.
